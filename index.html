Wrong Way Driving Project

The wrong way driving project is an example of a project I got to work on. The goal of this project is to prevent wrong way drivers on highway ramps. Methods used include detection cameras, signage, and illumanated warnings. I had the oppertunity to examine ramp and sign design, attend planning meetings, and visit sites included in the project. The images posted are examples of a wrong way driving prevention sign and an example ramp design on New Circle Road in Lexington. By the time my internship is done the project will be nearly complete, onlt estimations and desin conformations will remian. Long term, the goal is to implement these measures across the entire state.

Cumberland Gap Tunnel

One of the sites I got to visit was the Cumberland Gap Tunnel. Located in the Cumberland Gap National Historic Park, this tunnel provides 4 lane routes of U.S. route 25 to Kentucky, Tennessee, and Virginia. This is one of two mountain tunnels in the U.S. that crosses state lines. While at the site, I had the oppertunity to take a tour of the facility. This tunnel has in depth saftey measures with EMTs on staff along with a firefighting team. The tunnel also boasts chemical drainage systems, 2 backup power systems, and firehoses every 150 feet inside the tunnel. Another main feature of the tunnel is the chemical truck transport. The tunnel is designed to escort chemical cargo with the upmost saftey. All in all this visit was very educational and the tour provided by John Prater was excellent.
